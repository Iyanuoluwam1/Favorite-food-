FRIED RICE

Fried rice first developed during the Sui Dynasty in China and as such all fried rice dishes can trace their origins to Chinese fried rice.

Ingredient Measurement
Vegetable, or peanut oil ¾ cup
Onion ½ diced cup
Carrot 1 diced cup
Tablespoon Sichuan chili bean paste 2 teaspoon
Minced garlic 2 teaspoon
Minced ginger 2 teaspoon
Fresh or frozen peas ½ cup
Thinly sliced scallions 2 teaspoon
Rice (at least 1 day old) 4 cups
Regular soy sauce (preferably low-sodium) 2 tablespoons
Dark soy sauce 1 teaspoon
Beaten eggs 2

STEPS FOR MAKING FRIED RICE

  1. Heat oil over high heat in a large non-stick or well-seasoned pot.
  2. Add the onion and carrot and stir-fry for about 4 to 5 minutes until the vegetables are tender.
  3. Add the chili bean paste, garlic, and ginger and continue to cook for another 1 to 2 minutes until fragrant and golden.
  4. Mix in the peas and scallions and cook for another 1 to 2 minutes.
  5. Add the rice and mix to combine, allowing the rice to dry out in the wok a bit as you continue to stir, about 1 to 2 minutes.
  6. Add the soy sauces and stir-fry for another 1 to 2 minutes to combine.
  7. Push the rice to one side of the wok and add the beaten egg to the vacant side.
  8. Allow the egg to cook, occasionally stirring the egg to gently cook until it’s no longer runny.
  9. Then mix the cooked scrambled eggs into the rice to finish it off and serve immediately.
